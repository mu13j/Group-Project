What Wins Football Games: Offense or Defense?

Team Members: Sharon Parks, Joshua Mu, Kelvin So, Ram Chepuri, Corey Plott

Project Scope: Comparing the offensive and defensive stats for all NFL teams during the year for the last twenty years and analyzing how those stats yield wins.

Initial hypothesis: While the phrase “Defense wins championships, but offense wins games” is popular among the football community, our group is interested how accurate this statement is.

Data pulled: 1. Defensive stats allowed (y-axis): a. Turnovers allowed b. Passing yards allowed c. Rushing yards allowed and d. Points allowed 2. Offensive stats gained (x axis): a. Turnovers b. Passing yards gained c. Rushing yards gained and d. Points scored 3. Team wins (bubble radius)

Inspiring visualization: The final product, a motion chart like the one below, will show the offensive yards on the x axis, the defensive yards allowed on the y axis and the team wins will reflect the bubble radius.

Link to database: http://sportsdatabase.com/nfl

Github Repository: https://github.com/mu13j/Group-Project

Technologies used: Python, Flask, SQLAlchemy, SQLite, RESTful API, D3, Plotly, Bootstrap.

User interaction: Motion Chart, Searchable Table, Dropdown Menus
